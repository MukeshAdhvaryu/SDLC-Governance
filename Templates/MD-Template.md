## The Problem

What is the fundamental design problem this decision addresses?

A Major Decision document captures a design choice that is large enough to affect
multiple components, long-lived enough to outlast the current team's memory, or
controversial enough that its reasoning needs to be preserved.

State the problem plainly. Then state why the industry or conventional wisdom gets
it wrong, if that is the case. Cite evidence.

---

## Why Existing Approaches Fall Short

What does the industry, the language, or common convention do instead? What goes
wrong?

Be specific. Vague criticism ("it is not clean enough") is not a reason. A concrete
failure case that demonstrates the problem is a reason.

---

## The Correct Design

What does the right answer look like?

Show it. A code example, a diagram, a table - whatever makes the correct design
immediately visible. Do not just describe it; illustrate it.

---

## Positions

### Decision Owner's Position

What is the primary decision maker's view? What led them here?

### Deliberating Contributor(s)

What did other contributors argue? Where did they agree or push back?

Preserving deliberation prevents the same arguments from being re-litigated in
future. Record the strongest objections and how they were resolved, even if the
resolution was "accepted as a known trade-off".
